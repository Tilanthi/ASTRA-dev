## Major Addition: Test Case 6 - Genuine Discovery Test

### What Was Added

A complete new test case (Section 7) demonstrating ASTRA's capability for genuine discovery beyond knowledge retrieval. This addresses Referee3's critique that ASTRA showed "correct recovery of known results but NOT new discovery, new inference, or superior reasoning."

### Test Case 6 Content

**Problem**: Star formation threshold - What causes the observed N(H₂) ~ 10²¹ cm⁻² threshold?

**Five Phases of Autonomous Discovery**:
1. Blind pattern discovery (knowledge isolation mode)
2. Causal structure discovery (FCI algorithm)
3. Hypothesis competition (ranking competing explanations)
4. Novelty assessment (quantifying information gain)
5. Testable predictions (generating falsifiable predictions)

**Results**:
- Discovered 3 causal drivers correctly: Jeans mass, magnetic field, virial parameter
- Identified column density as proxy (not direct cause)
- Generated 4 testable predictions for future validation
- Validation status: SUCCESS

**Figure**: test06_genuine_discovery.png showing all phases of analysis

### Key Distinction: Two Modes of Operation

The paper now clearly distinguishes between:

1. **Validation Mode (Tests 1-5)**: Using real observational data to validate ASTRA's ability to correctly recover known astrophysical results

2. **Discovery Mode (Test 6)**: Demonstrating ASTRA's capability for genuine discovery beyond knowledge retrieval, operating in knowledge isolation mode and generating testable predictions

### New Statement: ASTRA's Role in Scientific Discovery

Added a new subsection "ASTRA's Role in Scientific Discovery" (Section 8.3) with the key message:

> "We would not expect to give ASTRA one of the big contemporary problems like 'resolve the nature of the Hubble Tension,' or 'tell us what dark matter is,' or 'prove that we live in a holographic universe,' and expect to get definitive answers in a computational Eureka moment. But ASTRA's discovery architecture, used alongside and by an experienced astronomer, has capabilities in discovery and inference that go beyond those of straightforward AI (large language models) and/or machine learning. ASTRA's role in science is to work alongside the experienced astronomer to analyze data and facilitate genuine discovery. ASTRA is therefore a tool to assist the astronomer, rather than a replacement for domain expertise."

### Qualifications

The paper appropriately qualifies the results:
- Test Case 6 used synthetic data with known ground truth
- Predictions require observational validation
- Definitive scientific discovery requires collaboration with domain experts
- ASTRA is presented as a tool to assist, not replace, astronomers
