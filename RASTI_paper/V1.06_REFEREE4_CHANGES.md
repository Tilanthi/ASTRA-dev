### Overall Verdict: Minor Revision
The referee found that "The manuscript has improved substantially. The AGI overclaiming has been eliminated, the scope and limitations section is honest and well-placed, and Test Case 6 is a genuinely valuable addition."

### Major Concerns Addressed

#### M1. Test Case 6 uses synthetic data - MORE PROMINENTLY FLAGGED
**Action taken**:
- Abstract now explicitly states "synthetic data with embedded causal structure" (first mention of Test Case 6)
- Section title changed from "Genuine Discovery Test" to "Discovery-Mode Operation on Synthetic Data"
- First paragraph of Test Case 6 states: "We use synthetic data with embedded causal structure---where the ground truth is known to the authors but not revealed to ASTRA"
- Added qualifier: "This is a controlled benchmark demonstrating discovery-mode capability, not an open-ended discovery claim"

#### M2. Hypothesis competition scores - NOW DEFINED
**Action taken**:
- Added hypothesis scoring formula in Methods section:
  ```
  Score(H) = 0.35×evidence_fit + 0.30×plausibility + 0.25×predictive_power + 0.10×simplicity
  ```
- Added important caveat explaining that H1 (causal) receiving highest score does NOT mean it's correct
- Clarified: "The hypothesis competition score ranking represents 'which hypothesis best fits the correlational data' not 'which hypothesis is true.'"
- Noted that ground truth shows column density is NOT causally responsible, and H1's high score is expected from correlational data alone

#### M3. Novelty scores - NOW DEFINED
**Action taken**:
- Added novelty score formula in Methods section:
  ```
  Novelty = exp(0.3×log(statistical_strength) + 0.3×log(unexpectedness) + 0.25×log(interpretability) + 0.15×log(consistency))
  ```
- Clarified that literature comparison is disabled during knowledge isolation mode
- Explained: statistical_strength = 1 - p-value; unexpectedness = 1.0 for blind mode patterns
